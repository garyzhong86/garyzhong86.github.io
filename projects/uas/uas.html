Motivation

  • Traditional stationary traffic camera provide coverage in limited locations and have a high infrastructure investment cost
  • The ad-hoc deployment of sUAS equipped with appropriate communication systems is capable of transmission video signal in real time in a much more portable way compared to stationary camera
  • Automated fight control for sUAS have been rapidly expanding and adapting the latest flight technologies:
    • GPS-based guidance
    • long-range wireless video transmission
    • Automated flight assistance
    • Ultrasonic obstacle avoidance system
    • Return home function in the even of emergency
  • The Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) has introduced the Part 107 (the Small Unmanned Aircraft Regulations) regulation, streamlining the deployment and testing of sUAS

Objective

identify and evaluate the feasibility potential benefits of using small UAS for traffic monitoring and incident management

Approach

  • Using commercial-off-the-shelf sUAS (e.g., DJI Phantom 2, DJI Phantom 4, DJI Mavic Pro, GeoBlue Surveyor 800) [gallery ids="100,92,99,104,98" type="rectangular" link="file"]
  • Retrofitting the sUAS with communication devices that is capable of transmitting data via commercial 4G/LET network.
  • High-level concept of using sUAS for traffic incident management [gallery ids="107,96" type="rectangular" link="file"]
