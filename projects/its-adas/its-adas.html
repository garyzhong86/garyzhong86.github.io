Virtual Guide Dog,
the next-gen personalized signal crossing assistant for the visually impaired

1. Motivations

  • Accessible pedestrian signal (APS) was proposed as a mean to achieve the same level of service required by the American with Disability Act (ADA) for the visually impaired (VIs)
  • VGD, a mobile-based APS application, was designed to provide VIs with personalized intersection crossing instructions for safe and prompt crossing.
  • VGD integrates geo-information and on-board sensor of modern smartphone (e.g., GPS, compass, accelerometer, and gyroscope sensor) to notify VIs for intersection crossing
  • VGD is able to place a pedestrian crossing call to the controller via wireless network (e.g., local Bluetooth or WiFi network, cellular network

Research Approach

  • The research team conduced proof-of-concept field test with the aid of hardware-in-the-loop simulation technique where a actual Econolite ATC controller was use
  • The controller is retrofit with Raspberry Pie with a Bluetooth sensor
  • A C# external program was written to retrieve the instant signal and call status of the controller via the National Transportation Communication for ITS Protocol

2. In-vehicle Queue Warning System

  • The Every Day Counts (EDC) Smarter Work Zone Program emphasize the use of ITS technologies to managed and mitigate the impact of work zone (e.g., lane closure, detours, lane narrowing etc.)
  • NJIT has deployed eight remote traffic microwave sensors (RTMS) in the proximity of the I-295 Direct Connect work zone in New Jersey.
  • A mobile QWS application was envisioned to unitize the existing sensors with the future integration plan for future NJDOT fleet
  • Proof-of-concept field test was conducted to test the integration of the QWS database and the mobile application
